History of Sedans

The term "sedan" originated in the 17th century, referring to an enclosed chair carried on poles, offering a comfortable mode of transport for the elite. In the early 20th century, the design evolved into the modern automobile sedan, characterized by a fixed roof and separate compartments for the engine, passengers, and luggage

History of SUVs

In the 1940s, the Willys Jeep and the Land Rover were among the first to combine off-road capability with passenger comfort, setting the foundation for the SUV category. The 1984 Jeep Cherokee (XJ) marked a significant milestone by introducing unibody construction in a four-wheel-drive vehicle, making SUVs more accessible and appealing to urban families .

History of Hatchbacks

The concept gained further prominence with the 1961 Renault 4, which combined the practicality of a hatchback with the comfort of a saloon car. Its success demonstrated the potential of hatchbacks for everyday consumers, leading to widespread adoption in Europe and beyond.
